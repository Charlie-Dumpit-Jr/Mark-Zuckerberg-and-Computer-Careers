Mark Zuckerberg

1984 - Present

Media Magnate - Internet Entrepreneur - Philanthropist

Here's a time line of Mark Zuckerberg's life:

  • 1984 - Born in White Plains, New York, United States.
  • 1990 - Zuckerberg began using computers and writing software in middle school. His father taught him Atari BASIC Programming in the 1990s, and later hired software developer David Newman to tutor him privately.
  • 2002 - Graduates from Phillips Exeter Academy, a private school in New Hampshire. He joins Harvard University in autumn on a psychology and computer science course.
  • 2003 - Zuckerberg met his future wife, fellow student Priscilla Chan, at a fraternity party during his sophomore year at Harvard. They began dating in 2003.

    • Projects and Accomplishments

  • 2004 - Starts Thefacebook.com in February. Drops out of Harvard at the end of his sophomore year. Later that year, Facebook hits 200,000 users. Contacts venture capitalists in Silicon Valley, and receives an initial investment of half a million dollars.
  • 2005 - Facebook hits 5 million users.
  • 2006 - At 22 years of age, Zuckerberg turns down an estimated $1 billion dollar offer for Facebook from Yahoo.
  • 2007 - Reaches a settlement with Winklevoss lawsuit worth $65 million, though still denies any intellectual property theft.
  • 2010 - Donates $100 million to the Newark, New Jersey, public school system.
